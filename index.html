An interesting problem to tackle is how art history is taught in schools. Students think of art history as a boring and difficult subject to learn since they need to memorize and understand facts, concepts, and historical events (Mz & Sy 2008). A solution to this problem is to improve how art history is taught by letting students experience the art itself during its creation, this is possible by developing a 3D game using Unity. Creating a game comprising the life of each artist that’s part of the syllabus in an art history class will take time since the developer would also need to study each era in art history. A game that would let students explore each era and experience how to create the artwork will allow students to learn through experience. This will be useful at some point in someone’s life who’s currently taking up an art history class. Since it is considered that art history is a boring and difficult subject to learn and a way to make it entertaining and easier to learn is to create a game-based application that would address the problems that students are encountering while studying art history.

The game could be composed of minigames, and the life of each artist would be discussed in every minigame. The minigames would be varying puzzle games depending on the artwork being discussed. It could be a game that would let you paint, sculpt, or a puzzle game that would help teach the life of the artist and the details of the artist's artwork. The game will also let you interact with the model of an artist as the NPC of each minigame, and let you learn as if being there with the artist. Each minigame would be separated into different rooms or areas and the design of each room and area would depend on where the artwork is created. As the game progresses, the player would be able to explore each era in chronological order as if being part of the era while playing the game. For example, a level of a game could be the life of Leonardo da Vinci then the room or area will be similar to where Leonardo da Vinci might have painted the Mona Lisa. An example playthrough is when the player starts the game, the player would be able to enter the first level of the game. Upon entering the first level, the player would encounter the artist of a specific era. The player would be able to talk to the artist and the player would need to solve a puzzle to move to the next level. The puzzle could be a painting game if the artwork is a painting, this would allow the player to experience how the painting is formed, what style is used, and techniques involved to complete the painting. After completing the minigame, the player will talk to the artist and there will be a dialogue about how the artist lived during the time and talk more about the artwork. This will be the same for every room or area, except for the type of minigame, artist, and design of the area or room. Upon completion of all the eras, the player is expected to learn about the life and artwork of every artist the player encountered in the game.
